Electric Railway System background information

The electric railway system was a groundbreaking innovation that emerged during the late 19th century, transforming urban transportation. Pioneered in Germany in the 1870s and spreading quickly across industrialized nations, electric trains replaced steam-powered locomotives by offering quieter, faster, and more efficient transit. With the development of overhead lines and third-rail systems, electric trains became the backbone of mass public transportation, enabling the rapid expansion of cities. Electrical engineers contributed by designing motors, braking systems, control units, and power infrastructure, all essential to building safe and scalable electric railway networks.

The Electric Railway System and its Impact on Tokyo's developemnt
